## **Creamy Coconut‑Chicken & Rice “Bowl” with a Crunchy Peanut Drizzle**

A quick, one‑pot meal that turns plain leftovers into a tropical‑savory feast.
The creamy coconut sauce gives the dish a silky texture, while the peanut drizzle adds a nutty crunch and a hint of heat.  It’s perfect for a week‑night dinner or a lunch‑box treat.

> **Why this recipe?**
> • Uses exactly what you have (chicken + rice).
> • Adds fresh veggies and herbs for nutrition.
> • The coconut‑peanut combo is a flavor twist that feels like a vacation.
> • One pan (or pot) cleanup—no extra dishes.

---

### Ingredients

| Item | Amount | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| Leftover cooked chicken (shredded or diced) | 2 cups | About 1‑1.5 lb cooked chicken |
| Leftover cooked rice | 3 cups | White, jasmine, or brown |
| Coconut milk (full‑fat) | 1 cup | 240 ml |
| Chicken broth or water | ½ cup | 120 ml |
| Red bell pepper | 1 | Diced |
| Carrot | 1 | Julienned or thinly sliced |
| Frozen peas | ½ cup | Thawed |
| Fresh cilantro | ¼ cup | Roughly chopped |
| Lime | 1 | Juice + zest |
| Garlic | 2 cloves | Minced |
| Ginger | 1 inch | Grated |
| Soy sauce | 2 Tbsp | Low‑sodium if you like |
| Brown sugar | 1 tsp | Optional, for balance |
| Red pepper flakes | ¼ tsp | Adjust to taste |
| Peanut butter (smooth or crunchy) | 3 Tbsp | 45 g |
| Rice vinegar | 1 Tbsp | 15 ml |
| Honey or maple syrup | 1 Tbsp | 15 ml |
| Water | 2 Tbsp | For the peanut sauce |
| Salt & pepper | to taste |  |

> **Optional extras** (pick one or two):
> • Sliced green onions, toasted sesame seeds, or crushed peanuts for topping.
> • A handful of spinach or kale, added in the last minute to wilt.
> • A splash of sriracha or a drizzle of chili oil for extra heat.

---

### Equipment

* Medium‑sized skillet or sauté pan (or a large saucepan).
* Small bowl for the peanut sauce.
* Wooden spoon or spatula.

---

### Instructions

1. **Prep the peanut drizzle**
   1. In a small bowl, whisk together peanut butter, rice vinegar, honey, and 2 Tbsp water until smooth.
   2. Set aside. If the sauce is too thick, add a splash more water until it reaches a pourable consistency.

2. **Sauté aromatics**
   1. Heat the skillet over medium heat. Add a splash of oil (olive or coconut).
   2. Add minced garlic and grated ginger; sauté 30 s until fragrant.
   3. Toss in diced red bell pepper and carrot; cook 2–3 min until slightly softened.

3. **Add the rice & chicken**
   1. Stir in the leftover rice and shredded chicken.
   2. Pour in coconut milk and chicken broth.
   3. Add soy sauce, brown sugar, and red pepper flakes.
   4. Bring to a gentle simmer, stirring occasionally.
   5. Let it cook for 5–7 min, allowing the rice to absorb the coconut‑soy mixture and become creamy.

4. **Finish the sauce**
   1. Stir in the thawed peas and half of the chopped cilantro.
   2. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
   3. If you like a little extra tang, squeeze in the lime juice and sprinkle some zest.

5. **Serve**
   1. Spoon the creamy mixture into bowls or plates.
   2. Drizzle the peanut sauce over the top.
   3. Garnish with the remaining cilantro, sliced green onions, toasted sesame seeds, or crushed peanuts if desired.
   4. Add a wedge of lime on the side for an extra burst of citrus.

---

### Tips & Variations

| Idea | How to Do It |
|------|--------------|
| **Make it a taco bowl** | Use the same base, but serve over a bed of lettuce or tortilla chips. Top with avocado slices, salsa, and a dollop of sour cream. |
| **Add a protein boost** | Stir in a handful of edamame or a fried egg for extra protein. |
| **Make it vegan** | Replace chicken with tofu or tempeh, and use a plant‑based coconut milk. |
| **Spice it up** | Add a tablespoon of sriracha or a pinch of cayenne pepper to the sauce. |
| **Use different grains** | Swap rice for quinoa, couscous, or even cauliflower rice for a low‑carb version. |

---

### Why This Works

* **Flavor balance** – The coconut milk gives a sweet, creamy base; soy sauce adds umami; lime and cilantro bring brightness; peanut sauce adds nuttiness and a subtle heat.
* **Texture contrast** – Soft rice and chicken meet crunchy veggies and a silky sauce.
* **Minimal cleanup** – One pan, one bowl for the sauce.
* **Versatility** – Works as a main dish, a side, or a lunch bowl.

Enjoy turning your leftovers into a tropical‑inspired, crowd‑pleasing meal!

## Coconut‑Curry Chicken & Rice Bake
*A one‑pan, oven‑baked dish that turns your leftover chicken and rice into a fragrant, creamy, and slightly sweet meal. The coconut milk gives the rice a silky texture, while the curry powder adds warmth and depth. Finish it with a splash of lime and a handful of toasted cashews for a bright, nutty crunch.*

> **Why this recipe?**
> • Uses what you already have (chicken & rice).
> • Requires only one baking dish—minimal clean‑up.
> • The coconut‑curry combo is a crowd‑pleaser and can be tweaked to suit your taste.
> • Works great for a week‑night dinner or a quick lunch.

---

### Ingredients

| Item | Amount | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| Leftover cooked chicken (shredded or diced) | 2 cups | About 1 ½ lb cooked chicken breast or thighs |
| Leftover cooked rice | 3 cups | Preferably cold or at room temp |
| Coconut milk (full‑fat) | 1 cup | 240 ml |
| Chicken broth (low‑sodium) | ½ cup | 120 ml |
| Mild curry powder | 2 Tbsp | Adjust to taste |
| Ground cumin | 1 tsp | Optional, for extra depth |
| Garlic, minced | 2 cloves | |
| Fresh ginger, grated | 1 tsp | |
| Red bell pepper, diced | ½ cup | Adds color & crunch |
| Frozen peas | ½ cup | Thawed |
| Lime, juiced | 1 Tbsp | Brightens the dish |
| Fresh cilantro, chopped | 2 Tbsp | For garnish |
| Toasted cashews | ¼ cup | For crunch |
| Olive oil or coconut oil | 1 Tbsp | For sautéing |
| Salt & pepper | to taste | |

> **Optional extras**
> • ½ cup diced pineapple (for a sweet‑tangy twist)
> • 1 Tbsp soy sauce or tamari (for umami)
> • 1 Tbsp honey or maple syrup (if you like a hint of sweetness)

---

### Equipment

* 9×13 in. baking dish (or any oven‑safe casserole dish)
* Medium saucepan (for sautéing)
* Mixing spoon or spatula
* Measuring cups & spoons

---

### Instructions

1. **Preheat the oven** to **375 °F (190 °C)**.
2. **Sauté aromatics**
   * In a medium saucepan, heat the oil over medium heat.
   * Add the minced garlic, grated ginger, and diced red bell pepper. Cook for 2–3 min until fragrant and the pepper is slightly softened.
3. **Add the curry**
   * Stir in the curry powder and cumin. Cook for 30 sec, letting the spices toast.
4. **Combine liquids**
   * Pour in the coconut milk and chicken broth. Bring to a gentle simmer, then reduce heat to low.
5. **Fold in the rice & chicken**
   * Add the leftover rice and shredded chicken to the saucepan. Stir until everything is evenly coated with the curry‑coconut sauce.
   * Season with salt and pepper to taste.
6. **Transfer to the baking dish**
   * Pour the mixture into the prepared baking dish, spreading it out evenly.
   * Sprinkle the thawed peas (and optional pineapple, if using) over the top.
7. **Bake**
   * Cover the dish with foil and bake for **20 min**.
   * Remove the foil, drizzle the lime juice over the top, and bake uncovered for an additional **10 min** (or until the edges are slightly golden and the liquid has thickened).
8. **Finish & serve**
   * Remove from the oven and let rest for 5 min.
   * Sprinkle chopped cilantro and toasted cashews over the top.
   * Serve hot, spooning the creamy rice and chicken into bowls.

---

### Tips & Variations

| Idea | How to Do It |
|------|--------------|
| **Spicier** | Add ½ tsp cayenne pepper or a dash of sriracha to the sauce. |
| **Vegetarian** | Replace chicken with cooked chickpeas or tofu cubes. |
| **Low‑fat** | Use light coconut milk and reduce the oil. |
| **Gluten‑free** | Ensure the curry powder is gluten‑free; use tamari instead of soy sauce. |
| **Make it ahead** | Assemble the dish, cover, and refrigerate for up to 2 days. Reheat in the oven at 350 °F (175 °C) for 15–20 min. |

---

### Why This Works

* **Flavor balance** – The coconut milk gives a subtle sweetness that balances the earthy curry powder.
* **Texture contrast** – The creamy sauce coats the rice, while the toasted cashews add a satisfying crunch.
* **Minimal cleanup** – One baking dish, one saucepan, and a few utensils.
* **Versatility** – Swap in any leftover protein or veggies you have on hand.

Enjoy turning your leftovers into a restaurant‑style meal that’s both comforting and exciting!
